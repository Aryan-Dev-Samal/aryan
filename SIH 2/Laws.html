Information Technology Act, 2000 - Indian IT Laws

Overview of Key Provisions

The Information Technology Act, 2000, provides the legal framework for e-commerce, digital signatures, cybercrime, and electronic records in India. Below are the important sections and amendments to the law:

  • Section 2: Definitions
    Defines terms like "computer," "data," "cybersecurity," and more.
  • Section 3: Digital Signatures
    Details the process of authenticating electronic records through digital signatures.
  • Section 4: Legal Recognition of Electronic Records
    Grants electronic records the same legal status as physical documents.
  • Section 6: Use of Electronic Records and Digital Signatures in Government
    Allows for the use of electronic communication with government authorities.
  • Section 10A: Validity of Contracts Formed by Electronic Means
    Recognizes contracts made electronically as legally valid.
  • Section 43: Penalties for Unauthorized Access
    Penalizes unauthorized access, downloading, or copying of data with up to ₹1 crore in compensation.
  • Section 43A: Compensation for Failure to Protect Data
    Requires companies to protect sensitive personal data, with compensation for failure.
  • Section 66: Hacking with Computer System
    Criminalizes hacking with penalties of up to 3 years in prison or a fine of ₹5 lakh.
  • Section 66B: Punishment for Receiving Stolen Computer Resources
    Penalizes knowingly receiving stolen computer resources with a penalty of up to 3 years imprisonment.
  • Section 66C: Punishment for Identity Theft
    Penalizes the misuse of someone’s identity information with up to 3 years imprisonment.
  • Section 66D: Cheating by Personation Using Computer Resources
    Penalizes online impersonation and fraud with up to 3 years imprisonment and a fine of ₹1 lakh.
  • Section 66E: Violation of Privacy
    Punishment for capturing or transmitting private images of a person without consent.
  • Section 66F: Cyber Terrorism
    Defines and criminalizes cyber terrorism, with a penalty of life imprisonment.
  • Section 67: Obscenity and Indecency in Electronic Form
    Penalizes the publication of obscene content electronically, with punishments ranging from 3 to 5 years imprisonment.
  • Section 67A: Publishing Sexually Explicit Material
    Severe punishments for transmitting or publishing sexually explicit material.
  • Section 67B: Child Pornography
    Severe penalties for publishing, browsing, or viewing child pornography.
  • Section 69: Powers to Issue Directions for Interception
    The government can direct interception, monitoring, and decryption of information for national security purposes.
  • Section 69A: Blocking of Websites
    Allows the government to block websites in the interest of sovereignty, integrity, and security.
  • Section 70: Protected Systems
    The government can designate certain computer resources as protected systems, with penalties for unauthorized access.
  • Section 72: Breach of Confidentiality and Privacy
    Penalizes unauthorized disclosure of private information obtained during official duty.
  • Section 72A: Disclosure of Information in Breach of Contract
    Penalties for service providers who disclose personal data without consent.
